Case Study: Complete Property Mapping Insight

See how Orthophoto Mapping, NDVI Vegetation Health, and Surface Modeling work together to give homeowners a complete understanding of their property.

1. Orthophoto — Accurate, Measurable Property Map

The orthophoto provides a distortion-free, top-down map of the entire property. This allows homeowners to measure distances, plan improvements, and document their land with precision.

Homeowner Benefits

  • Measure patios, fences, driveways, and landscaping accurately.
  • Document roof and yard conditions for insurance or resale.
  • Understand property boundaries visually.

Suggested Image: Orthophoto Overview

A high-resolution top-down map of the property.

Example: Orthophoto showing house, driveway, trees, and yard.

Suggested Chart: Area Breakdown

Shows square footage of lawn, house footprint, driveway, and patio.

Example: Bar chart with 4–5 labeled bars.

2. NDVI — Vegetation Health Analysis

NDVI reveals the true health of grass, trees, and landscaping by analyzing how vegetation reflects near-infrared light. This exposes stress long before it becomes visible.

Homeowner Benefits

  • Detect irrigation issues early.
  • Identify stressed or diseased areas of lawn or trees.
  • Protect expensive landscaping investments.

Suggested Image: NDVI Map

Color-coded vegetation health map (green = healthy, red = stressed).

Example: NDVI map showing stressed zones near the fence line.

Suggested Chart: Health Distribution

Percentage of lawn in healthy, moderate, and stressed categories.

Example: Pie chart with green/yellow/red slices.

3. Surface Model — Elevation, Slope & Drainage

The surface model shows how the land slopes and where water naturally flows or pools. This is essential for preventing drainage issues and planning improvements.

Homeowner Benefits

  • Identify low spots where water collects.
  • Plan patios, driveways, and landscaping with slope in mind.
  • Prevent foundation or yard flooding.

Suggested Image: Elevation Heatmap

Color-coded elevation map showing high and low areas.

Example: Red = high ground, blue = low ground.

Suggested Chart: Elevation Profile

Shows slope from back yard to front yard.

Example: Line chart showing a downward slope toward the house.

What This Means for Homeowners

When combined, these three deliverables give homeowners a complete understanding of their property—above and below the surface. You can plan improvements confidently, prevent costly drainage issues, and maintain a healthy landscape.

  • Orthophoto: Know exactly what you have.
  • NDVI: Know how healthy it is.
  • Surface Model: Know how it behaves.
